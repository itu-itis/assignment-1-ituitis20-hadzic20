Male Teacher School

On 16th March 1848, the first male teacher school of Turkey was founded in Fatih, Istanbul. It was opened to make education system in Turkey better, and is the origin of today's Çapa Science HighSchool. The Male Teacher School had 3 teachers, 1 cleaning staff and 1 security guard in the first times of its long history. In the first year of education, 30 studens were accepted to the school and the education period was determined as 3 years. The aim was to educate few but qualified teacher candidates. Scientific tools were bought from Europe and one day of each week was spent for laboratory studies. In 1869, the school was seperated in 3 parts to give more specified education for teachers of different stages of school, like elementary or middle school.

Female Teacher School

Girls over a certain age was not comfortable with getting education from male teachers, and there were no female teachers so girls could not take a proper education. To solve this problem on 26th April 1870, a new school for educating female teachers was opened.

Çapa Anadolu Teacher Highschool

After changing a lot of buildings, in 1951, the school was settled in the building that is currently used. In 1991 the school was renamed as Çapa Anadolu Teacher Highschool, and remained so until 2014.

Çapa Science Highschool

In 2014, the Ministry of Education decided to change the school's name into Çapa Science Highschool, and this change also made difference in the curriculum. The school that started as Male Teacher School in 1848 is currently Çapa Science Highschool and continues giving qualified graduates every year.
